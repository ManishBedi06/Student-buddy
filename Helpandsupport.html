Emergency Help and Support for Students

Sometimes students may face emergency situations or serious problems while studying. It is important to know who to contact and where to go so help can be received quickly.

Emergency Situations (Immediate Help)

If there is an emergency and someone is in danger, students should call 911 right away.

Call 911 for:

  • Serious injuries or accidents
  • Immediate danger
  • Fire

Emergency Phone Number: 911

When calling, try to stay calm and clearly explain:

  • What happened
  • Where you are
  • If anyone is hurt

Hospital (Emergency Medical Help)

For serious health emergencies, students can go to the main hospital in the Comox Valley.

Where to go:

North Island Hospital.

Emergency: 911

Students should bring their MSP card or health insurance information if available.

Non-Emergency Help on Campus

If the situation is not an emergency but still serious, students should contact campus support staff. The first place to go is Student Services.

This includes:

  • Feeling unsafe on campus
  • Needing urgent help but not a medical emergency
  • Reporting a concern

Where to go:

Student Services – North Island College (Comox Valley Campus),

Student Services will guide students to the correct department.

When Students Feel Stressed or Overwhelmed

College life can be stressful, especially for new students. If students feel anxious, stressed, or emotionally overwhelmed, they should talk to someone as early as possible.

Students can contact:

  • Counselling and Wellness Services
  • Student Services
  • Office of Global Engagement (for international students)

Talking to someone early can help prevent bigger problems later.

Where to go:

North Island College (Comox Valley Campus)
