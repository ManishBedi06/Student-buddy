First Things to Do

1. Get a BC ID (Service BC)

If you are living in British Columbia, getting a BC ID is very useful. A BC ID is an official photo identification card.

You can apply for a BC ID at Service BC

What to bring:

  • Passport
  • Study permit
  • Home Address

Location: Service BC - Comox Valley

2500 Cliffe Ave, Courtenay, BC V9N 5M6

Opening hours

  • Sunday Closed
  • Monday to friday- 9:00am to 4:30pm
  • Saturday Closed

2. Apply for a SIN Number

If you plan to work in Canada, you must apply for a SIN number (Social Insurance Number). You cannot work without it. Even part-time jobs require a SIN.

What to bring:

  • Passport
  • Study permit (with work permission)

Location: Service Canada - Comox Valley

130 19th Street, Courtenay, British Columbia, V9N 8S1

Opening hours

  • Sunday Closed
  • Monday 6:00am to 4:00pm
  • Tuesday to friday 8:30am to 4:00pm
  • Saturday Closed

3. Get Your Student ID Card

This is one of the third important things to do. Your student ID shows that you are a NIC student and are needed for many campus services.

  • Needed for library and student services
  • Used for exams or campus access
  • Helpful with student discounts

5. Open a Bank Account

Opening a bank account helps you manage money, receive payments, and pay bills. Most banks offer special student accounts. Some common banks near NIC include CIBC and RBC.

What to bring:

  • Passport
  • Study permit
  • BC ID (if you already have it)

6. Medical Services Plan (MSP)

The Medical Services Plan (MSP) is the public health insurance program in British Columbia. MSP helps pay for basic medical services such as doctor visits and hospital care.

Once approved, students will receive a health card, which they should bring when visiting a doctor or hospital.

Why MSP is important:

  • Covers doctor visits and hospital services
  • Required for long-term residents of BC
  • Help reduce medical costs
