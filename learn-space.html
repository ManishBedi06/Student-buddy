NIC-Comox Valley

Starting college at North Island College (NIC) in the Comox Valley can feel new and sometimes challenging, especially for students who are coming from another city or country. Campus life at NIC is calm, friendly, and focused on helping students feel comfortable and supported.
The Comox Valley campus is smaller than large universities, which makes it easier for students to find their way around and connect with others. Many students feel more relaxed in this environment because classes are smaller and the campus is not crowded.

Facilities and Services

Student Services Office

The Student Services office is in Discovery Hall that helps students with general questions about college life. Students can get help with registration, forms, and information about campus services. This is a good place to go if you are not sure who to ask.

Office of Global Engagement (OGE)

The Office of Global Engagement (OGE) is a support center for international students at NIC and is in Puntledge Hall, Room 108. The OGE team helps students with many important things, including courses, enrollment, program changes, acceptance or confirmation letters, and transcripts.

NIC Students’ Union (NISU)

The NIC Students’ Union (NISU) represents students and supports student life on campus. NISU works to make sure student voices are heard and helps create a positive campus experience.
They may organize events, activities, and social programs that help students meet others and feel more connected to the college. NISU also provides useful information for students about campus services and student life.

Student Housing – Tul’al’txw Student Housing Commons

The Tul’al’txw Student Housing Commons is the first on-campus housing at North Island College and opened in fall 2025. The housing is designed with inspiration from the K’ómoks First Nation, and the name tul’al’txw means “a house or place of learning.” The building has 217 beds, including nano and single units, studio units, shared quad units with private bedrooms, and two-bedroom family suites

Counselling and Wellness Support

Students who feel stressed, anxious, or overwhelmed can access counselling and wellness support at NIC. These services are available to help students take care of their mental health and overall well-being while studying.
Counselling services provide a safe and private space where students can talk about personal, academic, or emotional concerns. Wellness support may also include stress management tips, mental health resources, and guidance on maintaining balance between school and personal life.

Accessibility Services

Students with disabilities or learning challenges can receive support through accessibility services at NIC. These services are designed to help students succeed in their studies and have equal access to learning. Accessibility support may include extra time for exams, note-taking help, alternative formats for course materials, or other learning accommodation based on individual needs.

Career and Employment Support

North Island College offers career and employment support to help students prepare for jobs. These services can help students with resume writing, interview tips, and basic career planning. Career support is useful for students who want to work part-time while studying or who are preparing for jobs after graduation. Students can learn how to look for jobs, apply for positions, and understand what employers expect.
